First, the personal stuff...

First, I was born and raised in San Francisco, and I talk about it a lot. It's a key part of who I am, and I'm proud of my city.

Second, I'm currently a student at UCI. I'm not going to say the full name of my university because it's too long to type. I'm currently a double major studying both Chemistry and Molecular Biology & Biochemistry (which is one major by the way, and I will call it MB&B from now on because the full name is too long to type. In terms of which major I prefer, I would have to say chemistry, because I've spent a lot of time around chemists, and my research is currently in chemistry.

Third, I'm a member of Joe Patterson's lab at UCI, where I study the influence of protein surface chemistry on the nucleation, growth, and activity of metal organic frameworks. If you didn't understand that, that's okay! Just head over to my research page to learn more about what I do. I take research very seriously, not because I'm a workaholic, but because I really enjoy the work I do. I love the fast-paced everyday crisis of a research lab: I thrive off of the chaos it provides.

Fourth, and sort of related to third, I'm planning on becoming a professor some day and leading my own research lab. To this end, I plan on applying to graduate schools for biochemistry or chemistry in order to extend my love of research for a few more years.
